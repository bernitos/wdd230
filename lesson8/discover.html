Overview

Located in the southeast of Côte d'Ivoire, 116 km east of Abidjan. Capital of the South Como region, it is one of the closest towns to the Ghana border. The dominant people and customary owner of the land is a group of the Agni ethnic group, the Sanwi, belonging to the Akan group from present-day Ghana1. This city is part of the Sanwi kingdom, one of the oldest kingdoms in the country. The town of Mafere is on the lower Bia river. Administratively, the locality of Maféré is a capital of commune, sub-prefecture and department. The department of Maféré has an estimated population of more than 34,760 inhabitants. The city of Maféré belongs to the Comoé district and is mainly the capital of the South Comoé region, one of the 31 administrative regions of Côte d'Ivoire.
